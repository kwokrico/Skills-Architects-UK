# UK Project Management

For issue packs and transmittals, use `uk-deliverables-workstages`. For contract mechanisms, use `uk-tender-contract-administration`.

## When to Use This Skill

| Question type | This skill | Use instead |
|---------------|------------|-------------|
| Delivery plan and strategic brief | `uk-project-management` | — |
| Consultant selection and RACI | `uk-project-management` | `uk-deliverables-workstages` for issue-pack RACI |
| Risk register, VM, design reviews | `uk-project-management` | `uk-cost-consultancy` for cost risk |
| Client reporting and progress meetings | `uk-project-management` | — |
| Programme/budget monitoring | `uk-project-management` | `uk-construction-programme` for site sequencing |
| Dispute escalation | `uk-project-management` | `uk-tender-contract-administration` for contract claims |
| Contract variations / EOT | `uk-project-management` | `uk-tender-contract-administration` |

## Execution protocol

1. Confirm project classification (HRB/non-HRB), RIBA stage, and appointment scope.
2. Establish or update delivery plan aligned to [`uk-plan-of-work`](../../subskills/uk-plan-of-work/uk-plan-of-work.md) stage gates.
3. Map consultant appointments, RACI, and meeting cadence.
4. Maintain risk register with owner, mitigation, and review date; schedule VM workshops at stage gates.
5. Monitor programme and budget against baseline; escalate variances with options.
6. Route contractual decisions to CA/legal as appropriate — do not determine claims.

## Inputs required

- Project brief, business case, and strategic definition
- Consultant appointments and scopes
- Baseline programme and budget
- Risk register and issue log
- Client reporting requirements

## Outputs

- Project execution plan (PEP) or delivery plan update
- Risk register with mitigation actions
- Progress report (status, programme, budget, risks, decisions)
- Dispute escalation log with recommended route

## Halt rules

- Halt contractual determinations — route to contract administrator and legal counsel.
- Do not approve gateway submissions without named accountable dutyholders (HRB).
