# UK Heritage Impact Assessment Starter Checklist

Starter checklist for UK heritage and planning stakeholders to structure robust, proportionate Heritage Impact Assessment submissions.

## Scope
- Primary application: uk heritage impact assessment starter checklist decisions across UK practice.
- RIBA alignment: RIBA Stages 1-3.
- Coordinates design, statutory compliance, and consultant interfaces.
- Covers new-build and refurbishment unless the brief states otherwise.
- Supports ARB Code of Conduct expectations for competence and records.
- Aligns deliverables with client governance and assurance requirements.

## Inputs Required
- Client brief, budget, programme, and procurement route assumptions.
- Site and context data: planning constraints, surveys, and existing records.
- Fire, structural, and building services strategies from appointed consultants.
- Compliance baseline: Listed building and planning policy requirements with Building Regulations interface awareness.
- Risk register including design, programme, and commercial risks.
- Gateway and dutyholder assumptions under Building Safety Act 2022 and CDM 2015.

## Decision Framework
- Establish project stage intent and lock stage-specific decision criteria.
- Test options against statutory compliance before cost or aesthetics optimisation.
- Review buildability and temporary works implications with contractor input where available.
- Confirm information responsibility matrix and document control protocol.
- Trigger formal design risk reviews at each major stage gate.
- Escalate unresolved compliance conflicts to the project board with options and impacts.

### UK Framework Anchors
- Building Regulations / Approved Documents: Listed building and planning policy requirements with Building Regulations interface awareness.
- Building Safety Act 2022: maintain auditable design decisions and golden thread records.
- CDM 2015: coordinate hazard elimination/reduction and principal designer duties.
- RIBA Plan of Work: stage-based deliverables and decision gateways.
- Engage local planning authority conservation officer early
- Reference Historic England guidance and local plan heritage policy
- Map stakeholders: LPA planning case officer, conservation officer, Historic England (where relevant), National Amenity Societies, parish/town councils, local civic society, and heritage trusts

## Outputs/Deliverables
- Stakeholder map, evidence list, significance baseline, and impact summary
- Consultation log and draft mitigation strategy
- Stage-end decision log and actions with owner and due date.
- Updated programme dependencies and risk mitigation actions.
- Cross-discipline coordination record suitable for audit and handover.

## Risk Notes
- Missing stakeholder input can delay or weaken planning outcomes
- Evidence gaps in significance appraisal undermine proportionality
- Treat assumptions as temporary; convert to verified inputs before stage close.
- Keep contemporaneous records for commercial, safety, and dutyholder assurance.
- Escalate unresolved statutory uncertainty before information release.

## Quick Checks
- Identify decision authority: LPA planning case officer and conservation officer
- Confirm statutory consultees where applicable (Historic England, amenity societies)
- Confirm next review date and required attendees before closing the session.
- Confirm file issue status and superseded information controls are correct.
